How it began...


            


Does this look familiar to you? These photos were taken at a highly prestigious university in the department of geosciences. Truthfully though, they could have been taken just about anywhere a geological collection is being housed. This disorganization was what first prompted master's student Sarah Timm to look into what, if anything, could be done to enable better management of these collections, thus increasing their access, usability, and preservation. As a direct result, in 2011, EGEMS was developed.


Due to the increasing need, and fueled by the major efforts of prestigious scientific groups such as the National Science Foundation, in early 2012 ECLIPSE was founded, its mission: to provide simple software solutions to help institutions and individuals better manage their collections and materials.


An organized collection means easier access, easier access means more use, and more use means the potential for more funding. Bottom line is, the database is just the beginning of many benefits a well managed collections can provide. Below are some of the people reaping these benefits as a result of using EGEMS, click here to join them today!


   


Institutions using EGEMS


VIRGINIA TECH






EGEMS is currently being used by the University's Department of Geosciences, as well as the Department's Museum. Before EGEMS was implemented at Virginia Tech, the department had two separate databases for minerals and fossils with no prospect of expansion to other materials. Using EGEMS, they were able to combine databases, and have already entered information for other materials totaling the number of current records close to 10,000.



VIRGINIA MUSEUM OF NATURAL HISTORY





The database VMNH was using for their geological materials, prior to switching to EGEMS, made both the entering in of data and the retrieval of key information difficult. Sometimes search results returned a "0" though the record did exist. Now VMNH enjoys the user-friendly interface of EGEMS and is able to enter and search for crucial information both quickly and easily.
